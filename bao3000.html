BAO3000 - Bonded Assembly Oven

Final Cure Production Oven for BETA Technologies Bonded Aircraft Wings

Duration: January 2025 - June 2025 Role: Lead Mechanical Engineer Company: BETA Technologies

Project Overview

During my tenure at BETA Technologies, I led the design and construction of the BAO3000 production oven, a custom-built furnace capable of curing the company's newly developed bonded aircraft wing. This wing utilizes a structural epoxy adhesive that must be cured at 200+ °F for extended durations to achieve full strength.

Key Challenge

Build a full-scale oven large enough to accommodate a 50 ft × 8 ft × 8 ft aircraft wing while meeting AMS2750 Class 2 Type D furnace standards for aerospace production at a fraction of commercial costs ($250k–$500k).

Technical Requirements

Temperature Uniformity

Precise control across nearly 3,000 ft³ of volume within ±10°F tolerance per AMS2750 standards

Controlled Heating

Managed ramp rates to protect bond integrity during cure cycles

Cost Constraints

Deliver aerospace-grade capability at <10% of commercial alternatives

Certification

Meet all AMS2750 testing requirements for production use

My Approach & Skills Applied

System Conceptualization & Analysis

  • Conducted heat transfer calculations to size heating elements and airflow distribution
  • Selected structural materials (polyisocyanurate insulation, steel framing) to minimize heat loss and ensure safety
  • Developed comprehensive SolidWorks CAD models of oven and all subassemblies

Heating & Controls Design

  • Selected and integrated two modified 30 kW salamander heaters (60 kW total, 6,200 CFM)
  • Partnered with electrical engineer to replace stock contactors with externally mounted Silicon Controlled Rectifiers (SCRs), enabling:
    • Fine resolution temperature control via PID loop integration
    • Elimination of two major failure modes (contactor cycling)
    • Variable voltage control for precise heating curves
  • Implemented 32 thermocouples with Opto22 Groov EPIC data acquisition/control system
  • Created real-time monitoring interfaces using Node-RED and Groov View

Airflow & Uniformity Engineering

  • Designed adjustable trunk air supply system with louvered registers, dampers, and blast gates
  • Iteratively refined design based on thermal testing results until AMS2750 tolerance (±10°F) was achieved
  • Balanced airflow across 48-foot length to ensure uniform heating of entire wing assembly

Leadership & Cost Control

  • Directed semi-weekly design reviews with cross-functional stakeholders
  • Coordinated with materials engineers, controls engineers, and manufacturing technicians
  • Managed project budget and procurement to deliver at $27,322 total cost
  • Led electrical engineer reporting to me on SCR integration and control system

Problem Solving Examples

Non-Uniform Heating

Solution: Redesigned trunk ducting and balanced airflow using adjustable dampers and blast gates until temperature spread was within ±10°F specification.

Temperature Overshoot

Solution: Empirically tuned PID loop parameters through iterative testing to achieve stable control without oscillation.

Safety Concerns

Solution: Added redundant over-temperature controllers, door-mounted status lights, and coordinated safety review with BETA's safety officer and South Burlington Fire Marshal.

Functional Performance

The oven successfully cured BETA's bonded wing, proving the viability of the adhesive bonding process for future production aircraft. This achievement validated the novel manufacturing approach and enabled scalable production.

Key Components & Systems

Structural Assembly

  • Wall Panels: 20× modular panels, each with 2× 2" polyisocyanurate insulation sheets, steel studs and tracks
  • Door: Hinged panel with swivel caster wheels, grab latches, weather stripping, and status light
  • Frame: Steel structural frame with 80/20 aluminum mounting surfaces

Heating System

  • Heaters: 2× Dayton 784JJ2A 30kW salamander heaters (modified)
  • Ducting: Custom trunk system with adjustable registers and dampers
  • Power Control: External SCRs replacing stock contactors for variable voltage control

Control & Monitoring

  • Controller: Opto22 Groov EPIC PR1 Processor
  • I/O Modules: 3× ITM-12 thermocouple input modules, multifunction mixed signal module
  • Safety: ProSense SCU-3100 limit alarm with independent over-temp protection
  • Interface: Custom Groov View HMI and Node-RED dashboard for monitoring
