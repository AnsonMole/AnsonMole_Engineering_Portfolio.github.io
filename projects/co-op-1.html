Project Overview

Designed and supported the development of a large-scale production oven intended for final curing of bonded composite aircraft structures. The system was required to meet AMS2750 Class 2 Type D furnace requirements and support repeatable, production-level composite cure cycles.

---

My Role

  • Mechanical and systems engineering support across design, analysis, and integration
  • Worked under AMS2750 requirements and internal aerospace manufacturing standards
  • Collaborated with manufacturing, controls, and test personnel
---

System Requirements

  • Certified as an AMS2750 Class 2 Type D furnace (±10°F tolerance)
  • Capable of curing large bonded composite aircraft assemblies
  • Uniform airflow and thermal distribution across working volume
  • Closed-loop temperature control with over-temperature protection
---

Mechanical & Thermal Design

Oven Structure

  • Modular insulated wall panel construction using steel framing and rigid insulation
  • Large access door with gasketed sealing and safety interlocks
  • Designed for manufacturability and maintenance access

Heating & Airflow

  • Dual resistive electric heating units supplying controlled thermal energy
  • Supply air distributed through internal ducting trunks and louvered registers
  • Return air drawn from central regions to promote uniform circulation
  • Airflow tuning achieved through blast gates and dampers

Thermal flow analysis and physical airflow tuning were used to minimize hot and cold regions within the working volume.

---

Controls & Instrumentation

Temperature Sensing

  • Extensive network of K-type thermocouples distributed throughout the oven volume
  • Separate sensing strategies for oven control and part temperature verification

Closed-Loop Control

  • PID-based temperature control using real-time thermocouple feedback
  • Weighted temperature averaging used to reduce sensitivity to outliers
  • Silicon Controlled Rectifiers (SCRs) enabled continuously variable heater power
  • Hard over-temperature protection implemented per AMS2750 requirements

Replacing discrete contactor-based heating control with SCRs allowed smoother ramp rates, reduced thermal overshoot, and improved heater reliability during long cure cycles.

---

Certification & Testing

The oven was designed to pass all required AMS2750 certification testing, including:

  • Temperature Uniformity Surveys (TUS)
  • System Accuracy Tests (SAT)
  • Thermocouple calibration and instrumentation verification

Testing procedures ensured compliance across ramp, dwell, and stabilization phases required for production composite curing.

---

Results & Impact

  • Achieved production-capable composite curing at a fraction of commercial system cost
  • Enabled in-house curing of large bonded aircraft structures
  • Met stringent aerospace thermal uniformity and control requirements

The final system cost was orders of magnitude lower than comparable commercial alternatives while meeting the same certification standards.

---

Engineering Judgment & Lessons Learned

  • Airflow control was as critical as heater power for thermal uniformity
  • SCR-based control significantly improved temperature stability and system longevity
  • Designing for calibration, testing, and maintenance is essential for certified systems
